Morph, Video Art, 2020

In this video project I took an image of clouds which had been colored in blues and golds, and then I made multiples of the same image but with different perspectives until all the images morphed together. The idea came to me as I was imagining how images moved when under the influence of psychedelics.

Video Installation, Video Art, 2020

This early work uses video editing software other than Premier Pro. I got the idea from watching the music video "Someday" by Wevel, in which it shows various mundane objects being spun in a circle until it turned into a morphed image. I decided to use tiles as my images and made them move clockwise until the tiles became a moving picture.

Enhancement, Video Art, 2020

Note: This video has rapid cuts. Do not watch if prone to seizures.

A video made using unused footage from a previous project. It's called "Enhancement" after I read that seeing two images being displayed at the same time forces the brain to use both hemispheres. In a sense, it enhancese your brain's ability to use both sides.

Gregior, Animation, 2020

I created this animation after teaching myself Adobe Animate. I made all the animation, music and sound using only my mouse to draw everything. I didn't have much of an idea what the video would be about, but it was mostly a way to test my animation ablilites.

The Xtal Project, Video Art, 2020

A music video for Aphex Twin for a school video project. Even though most of music videos for Aphex Twin have video footage of cloulds, I wanted to make something that both incorporates clouds but also edited the footage to make it less and less recognizabe at the end.

Zoom, 3D Animation, 2020

One of the first video animations that I made using the 3D program Maya. The rendering time took longer than expected so I made due with the limited footage available. All of the sounds were made by me, except for the alarm. I wanted to make soemthing that expresses my feeling of learning through zoom.

3D World, 3D Animation, 2020

A little animated short using Maya. I thought about how someone could create a world all inside a 3D modeling program. I imagined what it would be like if someone in the program became self-aware seeing how everthing around him has been made using simple geometric shapes.
